B Cell Targeting Therapies

Comprehensive overview of therapies targeting B cells along their lineage — from pro-B cells to long-lived plasma cells. Created by Marwin Groener, MD with the help of Claude Opus 4.6 by Anthropic. Compiled March 2026. This overview includes many (but not all) B cell therapies that are currently used or under investigation. The CAR-T cell section is incomplete due to the large amount of ongoing clinical trials. Hyperlinks for diseases and status link to the appropriate paper or trials.gov page. Errors are possible. Make sure to independently verify the information of interest.

Monoclonal Antibodies — Anti-CD20

Target mature B cells (pre-B through memory). CD20 is absent on pro-B cells, plasmablasts, and most plasma cells — a key limitation for diseases driven by antibody-secreting cells.

Rituximab CD20 Chimeric IgG1, Type I First-in-class (1997). CDC > ADCC. Chimeric structure → immunogenicity risk (anti-drug antibodies). IV. RA, GPA/MPA, PV (approved). Off-label: SLE, SSc, IIM, Sjögren's, ANCA vasculitis, NMOSD Approved
Ocrelizumab CD20 Humanized IgG1, Type I Greater ADCC, less CDC than rituximab → fewer infusion reactions. IV. Relapsing MS, primary progressive MS (approved) Approved
Ofatumumab CD20 Fully human IgG1, Type I Binds distinct CD20 epitope (small extracellular loop). Potent CDC. SC self-injection. Relapsing MS (approved) Approved
Ublituximab CD20 Chimeric IgG1, Type I Glycoengineered (low fucose Fc) → enhanced ADCC. IV. Relapsing MS (approved) Approved
Obinutuzumab CD20 Humanized IgG1, Type II Glycoengineered; enhanced ADCC + direct cell death induction. Type II: does not relocate CD20 to lipid rafts → less CDC but superior direct killing. Superior B cell cytotoxicity vs rituximab in RA/SLE samples in vitro. CLL, FL, SLE (approved). Investigated: transplant desensitization, nephrotic syndrome Approved (onc) Approved (SLE)

Monoclonal Antibodies — Anti-CD19

CD19 is expressed more broadly than CD20 — from pre-B cells through plasmablasts and some plasma cells. This allows depletion of antibody-secreting cells missed by anti-CD20 therapy.

AgentTargetType / FormatUnique FeaturesDiseases / ApplicationsStatus
Inebilizumab CD19 Humanized, afucosylated IgG1 Afucosylation → enhanced ADCC. Depletes plasmablasts and some plasma cells beyond CD20 coverage. IV q6 months. AQP4+ NMOSD, IgG4-RD, gMG (AChR+ & MuSK+) — all FDA-approved. Investigated: SSc, MS Approved
Obexelimab CD19 + FcγRIIb Bifunctional IgG1 Novel mechanism: co-ligates CD19 with the inhibitory Fc receptor FcγRIIb → functional B cell silencing rather than depletion. Preserves B cells but suppresses activation. IgG4-RD, SLE Phase 2/3

Monoclonal Antibodies — Anti-CD38

CD38 is highly expressed on plasma cells and plasmablasts — the antibody-secreting compartment resistant to anti-CD20 therapy. Also expressed on NK cells and activated T cells, contributing to broader immunomodulatory effects and infection risk.

AgentTargetType / FormatUnique FeaturesDiseases / ApplicationsStatus
Daratumumab CD38 Fully human IgG1κ First-in-class anti-CD38. Multiple killing mechanisms: CDC, ADCC, ADCP, direct apoptosis. Also depletes CD38+ Tregs and NK cells. Most potent CDC inducer among CD38 mAbs. IV or SC. MM (approved). Off-label in 24+ autoimmune diseases: refractory SLE, membranous nephropathy, ANCA vasculitis, AIHA, ITP, transplant AMR, ME/CFS Approved (onc) Off-label (AI)
Isatuximab CD38 Chimeric IgG1κ Stronger direct pro-apoptotic activity (caspase/lysosomal pathways) than daratumumab. Less CDC. Can deplete CD38+ memory B cells, plasmablasts, and plasma cells. MM (approved). Transplant desensitization Approved (onc) Phase 2 (tx)
Felzartamab CD38 Fully human IgG1 Developed specifically for autoimmune/transplant indications. Primarily ADCC/ADCP; minimal CDC. Depletes PCs and NK cells. FDA Breakthrough Therapy + Orphan Drug Designations. Late AMR (kidney transplant), primary MN, IgAN Phase 2
CM313 CD38 Humanized IgG mAb Novel anti-CD38 with unique CDR sequence. Induces CDC, ADCC, ADCP. NEJM 2024: 95% platelet response in ITP, rapid onset (median 1 week). Dual mechanism: ADCC inhibition on platelets + long-lived PC clearance. Phase Ib/IIa RCT in SLE (2025). ITP (NEJM 2024), SLE (Phase Ib/IIa) Phase 1/2
Mezagitamab (TAK-079) CD38 Fully human IgG1 mAb Subcutaneous administration — unique among anti-CD38s. Developed specifically for autoimmune indications (MM development discontinued). Placebo-controlled Phase 2b in ITP showed dose-dependent responses. Phase 3 ITP trial initiated Feb 2025. Orphan Drug + Fast Track designations. SLE (Phase 1b), ITP (Phase 2b → Phase 3), IgAN Phase 3 (ITP)

BAFF / APRIL Pathway Targeting

BAFF and APRIL are TNF superfamily cytokines that promote B cell survival, differentiation, and antibody production. BAFF binds BAFF-R, TACI, BCMA; APRIL binds TACI and BCMA. Targeting these pathways modulates B cell homeostasis and plasma cell survival.

Anti-BAFF BAFF / BLyS

Belimumab Soluble BAFF Fully human IgG1λ mAb First biologic approved for SLE (2011). Reduces naïve and transitional B cells. Does NOT block APRIL. Does not directly deplete memory B cells or plasma cells. SLE, lupus nephritis (approved) Approved
Ianalumab (VAY736) BAFF-R Afucosylated human IgG1 mAb Dual mechanism: blocks BAFF signaling at BAFF-R AND depletes BAFF-R+ B cells via enhanced ADCC. NEJM 2025 publication in Sjögren's. Primary Sjögren's (Phase 2b+), SLE/LN (Phase 3 SIRIUS-LN) Phase 2b/3
Tabalumab Soluble + membrane BAFF Human IgG4 mAb Targets both soluble and membrane-bound BAFF (unlike belimumab). Failed primary endpoint in ILLUMINATE-1. SLE (failed Phase 3) Discontinued
Blisibimod Soluble + membrane BAFF Peptibody (peptide-Fc) Four BAFF-binding domains. Failed Phase 3 (CHABLIS-SC1). SLE (failed Phase 3) Discontinued

Bispecific Antibodies / T-Cell Engagers

Redirect patient T cells to kill target B lineage cells by simultaneously binding CD3 on T cells and a B cell/plasma cell antigen. Developed for hematologic malignancies; now being repurposed for refractory autoimmune diseases.

AgentTargetsFormatUnique FeaturesDiseases / ApplicationsStatus
Blinatumomab CD19 × CD3 BiTE (scFv-scFv) First approved bispecific (2014). Short half-life → continuous IV infusion. Rapid B cell depletion via T cell redirection. B-ALL (approved). Compassionate use: refractory ASyS (myositis/ILD) with rapid improvement; SSc (Nature Medicine 2026) Approved (onc) Compassionate (AI)
Epcoritamab CD20 × CD3 IgG1 bispecific (SC) Subcutaneous administration — unique among CD20×CD3 TCEs. R/R DLBCL, FL (approved). Not yet in autoimmune disease Approved (onc)
Glofitamab CD20 × CD3 2:1 format (2× CD20, 1× CD3) Two CD20 binding arms for enhanced avidity. Given with obinutuzumab pre-treatment to reduce CRS. R/R DLBCL (approved). Not yet in autoimmune disease Approved (onc)
Mosunetuzumab CD20 × CD3 IgG1 bispecific Outpatient-friendly (lower CRS risk). Available SC and IV. R/R FL (approved). Not yet in autoimmune disease Approved (onc)
Odronextamab CD20 × CD3 IgG4 bispecific IgG4 subtype (vs IgG1 for others). R/R DLBCL (EMA-approved) Approved (EU)
Teclistamab BCMA × CD3 IgG4 DuoBody Targets BCMA on plasma cells. Depletes long-lived PCs that escape anti-CD20 therapy. Reduces autoantibodies from PCs. Key agent for autoimmune repurposing. R/R MM (approved). Compassionate: refractory SLE (NEJM 2024), SSc with skin fibrosis improvement + ILD stabilization (Nat Med 2026) Approved (onc) Compassionate (AI)
Elranatamab BCMA × CD3 Bispecific mAb (SC) BCMA-targeting TCE. SC administration. R/R MM (approved). Not yet in autoimmune disease Approved (onc)
Talquetamab GPRC5D × CD3 IgG4 DuoBody Targets GPRC5D on PCs (and keratinized tissues → skin/nail toxicity). Alternative PC target to BCMA. R/R MM (approved). Not yet in autoimmune disease Approved (onc)
Linvoseltamab BCMA × CD3 Bispecific mAb BCMA-targeting TCE in development. FDA CRL for MM (2024). R/R MM Phase 3
JNJ-7965322 BCMA × GPRC5D × CD3 Trispecific Combines teclistamab + talquetamab targets in one molecule. May prevent antigen escape. Early data suggest reduced toxicity vs dual agents. MM (preclinical/Phase 1) Phase 1

CAR-T Cell Therapies

T cells engineered with chimeric antigen receptors targeting B lineage antigens. Originally developed for B cell malignancies, now achieving drug-free remission ("immune reset") in refractory autoimmune diseases after a single infusion.

Autologous CD19 CAR-T

KYV-101 (Kyverna) CD19 Autologous (fully human) Fully human CD19 CAR-T. First efficacy evidence in progressive MS (2024). CSF penetration confirmed. Progressive MS, lupus nephritis Phase 1
Relmacabtagene autoleucel CD19 Autologous CD19 CAR-T. Phase 1 open-label in SLE (EClinicalMedicine 2025). SLE Phase 1
CTA311 CD19 Autologous Presented at ACR 2025 across multiple autoimmune conditions. Multiple autoimmune diseases Phase 1
In vivo CD19 CAR-T
(mRNA LNP)		 CD19 In vivo generation LNP-delivered mRNA encoding CD19 CAR directly to patient T cells in vivo. No leukapheresis, no lymphodepletion, no manufacturing delay. Potentially paradigm-shifting for access and cost. Refractory SLE (NEJM 2025 case report). Also: Hunter et al., Science 2025 Phase 1
CT103A BCMA Autologous BCMA CAR-T directly targeting plasma cells. Anti-SRP necrotizing myopathy (PNAS 2024), AQP4+ NMOSD Phase 1
Descartes-08 BCMA RNA-based (transient) Non-integrating mRNA CAR → transient expression. Potentially safer (no insertional oncogenesis risk) but less durable. Myasthenia gravis (MG-001 trial) Phase 1b/2a

CTA313 CD19 + BCMA Dual CAR-T (autologous) Dual CD19/BCMA CAR-T. ACR 2025 data in active SLE. SLE Phase 1
Bispecific BCMA/CD19 CAR-T CD19 + BCMA Single bispecific construct Single CAR construct targeting both antigens. First trial in refractory gMG (2025): safe, no DLTs, significant clinical improvement. gMG, CIDP, MS, NMOSD (trials ongoing) Phase 1
Zamtocabtagene autoleucel CD20 + CD19 Tandem, non-cryopreserved Tandem CD20-CD19 targeting. Non-cryopreserved product. Sustained drug-free RA remission case report. RA (case report, RMD Open 2024) Early clinical
BAFF ligand CAR-T BAFF-R, TACI, BCMA Autologous Uses BAFF as the CAR extracellular domain → targets all cells expressing any BAFF receptor. Broad B cell + PC coverage. Outperformed CD19 CAR-T in preclinical lupus models. SLE (preclinical) Preclinical

Other B Cell–Relevant Therapies

Small molecules and biologics that modulate B cell function, target plasma cells, or address the downstream products of B cell activity without direct cell-surface targeting.

AgentTargetTypeUnique FeaturesDiseasesStatus
BTK Inhibitors — Functional B Cell Modulation (no depletion)
Fenebrutinib BTK (irreversible) Oral small molecule Inhibits BCR signaling → reduces B cell activation, proliferation, antigen presentation, cytokine secretion. Also affects myeloid cells via BTK. Does not deplete B cells. MS (Phase 3), RA, SLE (Phase 2) Phase 2/3
Evobrutinib BTK Oral small molecule CNS-penetrant BTK inhibitor. MS (Phase 3) Phase 3
Rilzabrutinib BTK (reversible/covalent) Oral small molecule Reversible covalent BTK inhibitor — may have improved safety over irreversible BTKi. IgG4-RD, pemphigus, ITP Phase 2/3
Remibrutinib BTK (selective) Oral small molecule Highly selective BTK inhibitor. CSU, Sjögren's Approved (CSU)
Proteasome Inhibitors — Plasma Cell Apoptosis
Bortezomib 26S proteasome IV/SC small molecule Induces apoptosis in plasma cells (high protein synthesis / UPR dependency). Depletes short-lived and some long-lived PCs. Limited by neurotoxicity and hematotoxicity. MM (approved). Off-label: refractory SLE, LN, TTP, AMR Approved (onc) Off-label (AI)
FcRn Inhibitors — Accelerated IgG Catabolism
Efgartigimod FcRn Human IgG1 Fc fragment Blocks neonatal Fc receptor → accelerates IgG degradation → rapid reduction in total and pathogenic IgG. Does NOT deplete B cells — removes their antibody products. Now approved for gMG and CIDP. ALKIVIA Phase 2/3 met primary endpoint in IIM (TIS 50.45 vs 35.65, P=0.0004). gMG, CIDP (approved). Investigated: ITP, pemphigus, IIM/myositis (ALKIVIA Phase 2/3 — primary endpoint met), Sjögren's (Phase 2) Approved Phase 2/3 (IIM)
Rozanolixizumab FcRn Humanized IgG4 mAb Anti-FcRn mAb. SC injection. gMG (approved) Approved
Nipocalimab FcRn Human IgG1 mAb (effector-null) Anti-FcRn mAb. Effector-null IgG1. Minimal transplacental transfer → safest FcRn option in pregnancy. Continuous q2wk dosing = "longest sustained disease control" among FcRn class. JASMINE Phase 2 in SLE met primary endpoint (2026). Also investigated for maternal-fetal indications. gMG, Sjögren's, warm AIHA, HDFN, SLE (JASMINE Phase 2 — met primary endpoint) Approved (gMG)
IMVT-1402 FcRn Fully human IgG1 mAb (2nd gen) Next-generation FcRn inhibitor. Designed to achieve deeper IgG reduction (~74%) than 1st-gen agents while avoiding albumin/LDL effects (batoclimab liability). SC self-injection. Registrational programs in 5+ indications. Immunovant lead asset (batoclimab deprioritized despite positive Phase 3). gMG, CIDP, Graves' disease (Phase 3), D2T RA, Sjögren's Phase 2/3
